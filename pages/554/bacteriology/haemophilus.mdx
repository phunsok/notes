# Haemophilus

- small, Gram-negative, pleomorphic bacteria
- require enriched media containing blood or derivatives
- most are normal flora of mucous membranes; occassionally causes disease
- *Haemophilus influenzae* type b - important human pathogen
- *H. ducreyi* causes chancroid, an STD

## Habitat
- *H. influenzae* normal flora of mucous membrane of upper respiratory tract

## Pathogenesis
- *H. influenzae* causes meningitis in children, respiratory tract infections in children and adults

## Morphology
- *H. influenzae* are short (1.5Âµm) **coccobacilli**, sometimes in pairs and short chains; depends on age and medium - at 6-8 hrs in rich medium, coccobacilli forms are dominant; later longer rods, lysed bacteria and pleomorphic forms occur
- Definite capsules occur in young cultures on enriched media

## Culture
- require X (hemin) and V (NAD) growth factors
- does not grow on sheep blood agar, BA (unless around staphylococci: satellitism)
- On chocolate agar, CA: flat, grayish brown, 1-2mm in diameter,
